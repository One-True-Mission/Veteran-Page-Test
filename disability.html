Disability Resources for Veterans

Everything below is an official government resource (VA, DoD, DOL, SSA, or state). Each item includes a short description so you know exactly what you’ll find when you click.

Eligibility

What Counts as a Service-Connected Disability

A condition that was caused or made worse by your military service. Examples: physical injuries, hearing loss, TBI, PTSD, and chronic illnesses.

  • VA — Eligibility Overview

    Defines service-connected disabilities, presumptive conditions, and who qualifies. Good starting point before you file.

Apply

How to Apply for VA Disability Benefits

Typical steps: gather records and evidence, file your claim online (or through a VSO), complete any C&P exam, then review your decision letter.

  • VA.gov — File a Disability Claim

    Official online application. Secure portal to start, upload evidence, and submit your claim.

  • VA Form 21-526EZ

    Paper form for disability compensation. Use if you prefer to file by mail or need to review requirements line-by-line.

  • Check Claim or Appeal Status

    Track where your claim is in the process and see any requested actions.

  • eBenefits (VA/DoD)

    Legacy portal tied to VA/DoD records — helpful for document access, status checks, and service information.

Ratings & Compensation

Understanding Ratings & Compensation

VA assigns a rating from 0–100% in 10% steps. If you have multiple conditions, the VA uses a combined ratings calculation — it’s not simple addition.

  • Current VA Compensation Rates

    Monthly payment tables with amounts based on rating and dependents. Updated annually.

Decision Reviews & Appeals

If You Disagree with a Decision

Three main paths: Higher-Level Review (a fresh look, no new evidence), Supplemental Claim (submit new/relevant evidence), or an appeal to the Board of Veterans’ Appeals.

  • VA — Decision Reviews & Appeals

    Explains each option, timelines, and how to file. Use this to choose the path that fits your situation.

Independent Living & Accessibility

Adapted Housing Grants

Grants to modify your home (ramps, wider doors, accessible bathrooms) if a service-connected disability limits mobility.

  • VA — Housing Assistance for Disabled Veterans

    Explains SAH/SHA grants, eligibility, and how to apply.

Automobile Allowance & Adaptive Equipment

One-time payment for a specially equipped vehicle and/or ongoing assistance for equipment like power steering/braking adaptations.

  • VA — Auto Allowance & Adaptive Equipment

    Eligibility rules, covered equipment, and steps to apply.

VR&E (Vocational Rehabilitation & Employment)

Career counseling, training, and job placement for veterans with service-connected disabilities who need support returning to work.

  • VA — VR&E Overview

    How to apply, who’s eligible, and the services VR&E can provide.

Jobs & Training

Employment & Training for Disabled Veterans

Programs and protections that help disabled veterans prepare for, find, and keep employment.

  • U.S. Department of Labor — VETS

    Job resources, training opportunities, and information on workplace rights and accommodations.

State-Level Help

Free Claims Help in Your State

Most states have accredited counselors who file claims and appeals at no cost.

  • Texas Veterans Commission — Claims & Compensation

    Examples of what a state office can do: gather evidence, file/track claims, and help with decision reviews.

Other Benefits

Social Security & VA Disability

VA disability and Social Security Disability are separate programs. Your VA rating doesn’t guarantee SSDI/SSI, but you may qualify for both.

  • SSA — Information for Veterans

    Explains how to apply for SSDI/SSI, expedited processing for some veterans, and how SSA looks at medical evidence.

Directories

Find Local & National Resources

Search trusted programs by topic and location.

  • National Resource Directory (VA/DoD/DOL)

    Interagency database of federal, state, and community resources for veterans, caregivers, and families.

  • eBenefits

    Manage your benefits, upload documents, and access your VA/DoD records from one place.

  • Military OneSource — VA Benefits Overview

    Plain-language guides to VA benefits and links to additional support for families and caregivers.
